Experiment summary

Model assumptions

This is a deterministic teaching model, not a parameter-fitting tool. Adsorption follows mass-action loss of free phage against the susceptible host fraction. Productive infection follows a Poisson occupancy model, so multiple adsorptions to the same cell do not create additional burst events. Virucide only reduces free, unadsorbed phage. After the adsorption/virucide step, secondary infection is suppressed to mimic a one-step growth experiment.

The burst-time dial is treated as the first-release latent period. The rise to plateau is smoothed over a short rise period so the graph looks like experimental data rather than a perfect step function.
